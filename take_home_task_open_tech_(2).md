# Modo Energy Take-Home Task: Build and Demo

**Time:** 2–4 hours  
**Deadline:** 11:59:59 EST 11 March 2026  
**Submission Format:** GitHub repo

---

## The Brief

Pick a problem that matters to participants in electricity markets - traders, asset owners, developers, utilities - and build something that helps them understand the markets better.

That's intentionally open-ended. We're not prescribing a format or a market. But we are looking for something tangible: an app, a dashboard, a model, a tool. Something you can point at and say "Here's what I've built". Something you could put in front of a user to get feedback on. If you're unsure what kinds of problems Modo Energy's customers care about, a quick look at our product offering will give you a feel for the space.

### Inspiration

To give some structure to the open-endedness, here are some pointers. These are starting points, not constraints.

**Data sources** - you're free to use anything *publicly* available, but two good places to start are:

- **NYISO Market Information System (MIS):** [https://www.nyiso.com/market-data](https://www.nyiso.com/market-data) - load, capacity, pricing, and ancillary services data across New York's electricity markets
- **ERCOT:** [https://www.ercot.com](https://www.ercot.com) - a good place to start exploring; the [grid info](https://www.ercot.com/gridinfo) section in particular has generation, load, settlement point prices, and ancillary service data for the Texas grid

The best submissions tend to have a clear question or problem statement that drives the direction.

90% of Modo's modeling work is written in Python; that's what the team is most familiar with and the language you'll be working in most frequently. As such, we would recommend showcasing your ability in Python at some level; however if you'd prefer to complement this with other tools, or use something else entirely, go for it.

## How We'll Evaluate It

We're looking for someone who can define a problem worth solving, scope it to what's achievable in a few hours, and ship something they're proud of. We're also interested in **how you work**, not just what you produce.

**Use of AI is not just allowed - it's expected.** We want to see how you use AI tools to move faster, go further, and build things you couldn't build as quickly on your own. Show us your workflow, not just the output.

Beyond that, we're looking for:

- A clear point of view - why did you pick this, what were you trying to find out?
- Sensible scoping - did you make smart choices about what to build in the time available?
- Quality of thinking - is the analysis or output defensible and clearly communicated?
- Energy market awareness - does it reflect an understanding of how these markets actually work?
